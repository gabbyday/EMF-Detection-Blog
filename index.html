What is EMF Radiation?

Anything that has electrical charges produces electromagnetic field (EMF) radiation. EMFs are a natural phenomenon that have existed long before humans. However, with the rise in electronic device usage, humans are now more exposed to varying degrees of EMF radiation [1].

We have established that EMFs are electromagnetic fields. Now what exactly does that mean? Electromagnetic fields are formed from the propogation of orthogonal electric and magnetic fields. Think of them like the ripples that spread when you drop a pebble into water. These invisible waves carry energy and information through the air, allowing our devices to communicate wirelessly.

Different metrics are used to measure both. Let's assess the two [1]:

  • Electric Fields:
    • Created by differences in voltage
    • The higher the voltage, the stronger the electric field
    • Units of volts per meter [V/m]
  • Magnetic Fields:
    • Created when electric current flows
    • The higher the current, the stronger the magnetic field
    • Units of micro teslas [uT]

It is important to note that there are other metrics used to measure EMF radiation levels such as [ADD]. Due to the nature of my measurement device, I will be focusing on just the electric and magnetic field strengths.

Is all EMF radiation bad? When people think of radiation, it is easy to think only of the nuclear waste kind. In fact, there is a whole spectrum of EMF radiation to consider. Even humans emit EMF radiation! There are two types of EMF radiation that we can classify [2]:

  • Non-Ionizing: Low to mid-level frequency radiation which is generally perceived as harmless to humans.
  • Ionizing: High-level frequency radiation that may cause cellular and DNA damage.

https://www.niehs.nih.gov/health/topics/agents/emf/index.cfm

Commonly used devices operate at different ionizing levels. Most devices that humans are exposed to daily emit non-ionizing radiation.

How do EMFs Influence the Human Body?

Both electric and magnetic fields are capable of inducing voltages and currents in the human body. The human body relies on electric charges to function through biochemical reactions. For example, our brain activity, digestive behavior, and heartbeat are all electrically active. The strength of the induced current and voltage depends on the intensity of the EMF radiation exposure.

Now the question is, are they dangerous enough to create adverse effects on the body? Heating is the main biological effect of EMF fields. To date, scientists and regulatory agencies agree that low-frequency EMFs pose little danger to human health. The human body is extremely sophisticated and has mechanisms within to adjust to the various influences of our environment. With that being said, it is admitted that more research is needed especially for long-term exposure. Stress on our biological system for a prolonged period of time may contribute to a health risk [1]. Just think about how close your phone is to your body at all points of the day!

But what about all of these research findings that say otherwise?

  • National Toxicoloy Program:
    • Findings: A two-year study on mice found that there is clear evidence of an association with high exposure to cellphone radiation (900MHz) with tumors and DNA damage. [https://ntp.niehs.nih.gov/whatwestudy/topics/cellphones]
    • Credibility: This program is under the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services.
  • Environmental Health Trust:
    • Findings: Insurers range 5G and EMF radiation as a “high” risk, comparable to lead and asbestos.
    • Credibility: Findings are based off a 2019 study from Swiss Re Institute, a reinsurance company. [https://ehtrust.org/key-issues/electromagnetic-field-insurance-policy-exclusions/]
  • Independent Scientific Research:
    • Findings: Symptoms of ill-health were reported for electric field exposure of 10 V/m.
    • Credibility: This is a published research paper on Google Scholar. [https://www.scielosp.org/article/ssm/content/raw/?resource_ssm_path=/media/assets/bwho/v88n12/v88n12a09.pdf]

As you can probably notice, there are a ton of inconsistencies regarding EMF effects on the human body. Several of such claims were analyzed in a systematic review. Many of them were found to have issues with the way experiments were conducted. It was concluded that there were likely no concerning physical effects on the body. [https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6805477/].

It is important to note that this is just one review and there are approximately 25,000 articles that have been published over the last 30 years on the topic of non-ionizing EMF radiation on the body [cite]. This is why when reviewing content, it is crucial to cross-check with various sources and ensure that you are seeking information from credible publishers.
